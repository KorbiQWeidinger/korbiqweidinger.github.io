Technische Universität München

I am currently (01.2022) finishing my B.Sc. in Computer Science at TUM and am accepted to start my M.Sc. in Computer Science this summer (05.2022). I discovered my passion for programming in an orientation course at TUM in 16/17 were we used python and a raspberry pi. I started my B.Sc. in Computer Science in 17. There are some things I believe to be worth mentioning regarding my studies at TUM. In winter 19/20 I participated in the iInternship where I was taught swift for iOS development and was part of a team of 7 students that developed an iOS app for ZEISS. I afterwards chose to be a tutor teaching swift to fellow students and gave a presentation on how to develop a secure login service with server side swift. In winter 20/21 I was a tutor for functional programming and verification. More on my love for teaching here.

Anarcon

After my first semester in my B.Sc. in Computer Science at TUM I started my first job related to my career at the consulting company anarcon. I was very fortunate to be part of the creation of a team of developers where I learned how to set up a scrum team, create a build and deployment pipline for continuous integration with the atlassian products. Special thanks go to head of the team Alex who taught me a lot about programming, system design and how to work in an agile team. As a team we developed various plugins for Jira and Confluence which additionally taught me a lot about the Atlassian products. For my final tasks at anarcon I developed two plugins for Jira by myself.

CQSE

After two years at anarcon I wanted to experience something new and decided to follow my love for programming and clean code. I was happy to get a job at CQSE were I was eager to learn more about Continuous Quality in Software Engineering. I spent most of my time developing for their Software Intelligence Platform Teamscale which taught me a lot about quality metrics in different programming languages. I was also fortunate to write my bachelor thesis about the impact of static analysis on code quality in OSS projects at CQSE. You can find out more about my Bachelor Thesis here.
